Ultrafast Laser Optics: Auto-Correlator and FROG

Aug 2022 - Mar 2023

Associated with Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay

Project Overview

This project, part of the Summer Undergraduate Research Project at IIT Bombay, focuses on developing prototypes for Frequency-Resolved Optical Gating (FROG) and Auto-Correlator devices. The project is conducted under the guidance of Professor Dipanshu Bansal in the Vibrational Spectroscopy Lab, Mechanical Engineering Department.

Research Focus

The primary aim of the project is to develop advanced laser measurement tools for the Indian market, specifically focusing on miniaturizing the FROG and Auto-Correlator devices to make them market-ready. These devices are critical for analyzing ultrafast laser pulses, with applications in spectroscopy, material science, and other research fields.

Technologies and Equipment Used

  • Optical instruments (FROG, Auto-Correlator)
  • Laser pulse analysis
  • Opto-mechanical systems
  • Prototyping and miniaturization

My Role

My role in the project involved prototyping the Auto-Correlator and FROG devices, ensuring that they were functional for ultrafast laser measurement. I also contributed to the miniaturization process, which is crucial for making the devices product-ready. In addition, I explored opportunities to develop and distribute ultrafast laser equipment tailored to the Indian market.
