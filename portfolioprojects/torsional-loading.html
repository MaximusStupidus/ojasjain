Analysis of Non-Circular Members Subjected to Torsional Loading

Jan 2023 - Apr 2023

Associated with Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay

Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the ME202: Strength of Materials course. We explored the application of St. Venant Stress Theory and Prandtl Stress Function on non-circular geometries such as squares and ellipses. The study also delves into the potential for expanding this analysis to more complex shapes like airfoils.

Research Focus

The project investigates the behavior of non-circular members under torsional loading. Unlike circular members, non-circular geometries experience warping and non-uniform stress distributions, making their analysis more complex. This research applies classical stress theories to provide insights into the torsional rigidity and stress distribution in non-circular cross-sections.

Technologies and Theories Applied

  • St. Venant Stress Theory
  • Prandtl Stress Function
  • Application to non-circular geometries (squares, ellipses)
  • Exploration of extensions to airfoils

My Role

I contributed to the mathematical formulation and computational analysis of torsional stress in non-circular geometries. The project involved applying classical theories to modern engineering problems and preparing a comprehensive paper on our findings and future work.
